# Schola Arcana

A self-directed curriculum combining **mathematical logic** and **quantum computing foundations**, designed for rigorous study toward master's-level research in theoretical computer science and mathematical logic.

## what is this?

Schola Arcana is a documented path through seven interconnected tracks of pure mathematics and theoretical physics. Each track builds on the previous. 

The curriculum spans:
- **Linear Algebra Foundations** — vector spaces, eigenvalues, unitary operators
- **Quantum Computing Foundations** — qubits, entanglement, Bell inequalities, quantum algorithms, quantum information theory
- **First-Order Logic & Incompleteness** — soundness, completeness, compactness, Gödel
- **Modal & Epistemic Logic** — Kripke frames, knowledge, dynamic epistemic logic
- **Set Theory** — ordinals, cardinals, AC, Zorn's lemma
- **Model Theory** — elementary equivalence, types, quantifier elimination
- **Proof Theory** — sequent calculus, cut elimination, normalization

## why this exists

This curriculum serves dual purposes:

1. **Personal study path** — a self-directed deep dive into logic and quantum foundations for someone pursuing a master's in Quantum Computer Science,  Mathematical Logic or Theoretical Computer Science.
2. **Public reference** — documentation of a rigorous, structured approach to these topics for anyone building similar foundations

## structure

The website contains:
- **math_logic.html** — full curriculum map with all tracks, phases, topics, and reading lists
- **topics.html** — topical index (coming soon)
- **vault.html** — proof vault with LaTeX entries (coming soon)
- **journal.html** — study notes and reflections (coming soon)
- **index.html** — landing page

Each track is broken into phases. Each phase has:
- Reading assignments (textbook chapters)
- Topic checklist (mark progress)
- Key concepts with citations
- Milestones (end-of-phase summary)
- Proof vault targets (contributions to the vault)

## primary texts

- Enderton, *A Mathematical Introduction to Logic*
- Axler, *Linear Algebra Done Right*
- Nielsen & Chuang, *Quantum Computation and Quantum Information*
- Ronald de Wolf, *Quantum Computer Science* (lecture notes)
- Blackburn, de Rijke & Venema, *Modal Logic*
- Enderton, *Elements of Set Theory*
- Marker, *Model Theory*
- Troelstra & Schwichtenberg, *Basic Proof Theory*




## how to use this

1. **As a student:** Follow the curriculum. Click phases to expand. Check off topics as you complete them. Contribute to the proof vault.
2. **As a reference:** Use the topical index to find coverage of specific concepts.
3. **As inspiration:** This is one person's path. Adapt it to your own.
